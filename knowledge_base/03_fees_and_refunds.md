# Fees and Refunds

## Monthly Maintenance Fees

- **Basic Checking**: $12/month. Waived with direct deposit of $500+ or average daily balance of $1,500+.
- **Premium Checking**: $25/month. Waived with combined balances of $15,000+ across all accounts.
- **Savings**: $5/month. Waived with minimum balance of $300.
- **Business Checking**: $20/month. Waived with average balance of $5,000+ or 50+ monthly transactions.

## Overdraft Fees

Overdraft fee is $34 per transaction, with a maximum of 3 overdraft fees per day ($102 max daily). Transactions of $5 or less are not charged an overdraft fee. Customers enrolled in overdraft protection linked to a savings account are charged $12 per transfer instead.

To opt out of overdraft coverage for debit card and ATM transactions, customers can contact us or update settings in online banking. Checks and ACH transactions are always subject to overdraft processing.

## ATM Fees

- Bank-owned ATMs: Free for all account holders
- Non-bank ATMs (domestic): $3.00 per transaction. Premium Checking customers receive 4 fee rebates per month.
- International ATMs: $5.00 per transaction plus 3% foreign transaction fee

## Wire Transfer Fees

- Domestic incoming: Free
- Domestic outgoing: $25
- International incoming: $15
- International outgoing: $45

## Refund Policies

Fee refunds are handled on a case-by-case basis with the following guidelines:
- **First-time overdraft**: Automatically refunded as a one-time courtesy for customers in good standing
- **Monthly fees**: Can be refunded for the current month if the customer changes to a qualifying status
- **ATM fees**: Non-refundable unless caused by a bank error
- **Wire fees**: Non-refundable once the wire has been initiated

Representatives can issue refunds up to $100 without supervisor approval. Refunds between $100 and $500 require supervisor approval. Refunds above $500 require branch manager authorization.

## Fee Dispute Process

If a customer disputes a fee, document the dispute in the account notes, explain the fee structure, and attempt resolution. If the customer remains unsatisfied, escalate to the branch supervisor. All fee disputes must be resolved or escalated within 3 business days.
